About Me

My Background

Growing up in a small, rural, and peaceful town, I used to be fascinated with scientific innovation shows on the TV. From there, my interest in science and engineering grew. My childhood dream is actually to be a scientist, but that was because i did not know much about the world yet, hence I associate innovation and invention job with scientist. I also believe that i am blessed with good brain, making me to be able to keep up with those A students ( well, just manage to keep up, not overtake hehe) This allowed me to be in a Science-stream class until the end of my STPM. From there, I decided to go into engineering course as it feels closer to what i want.

However, the process of getting my foot into the engineering world was not easy. As a kampung boy, money is always something that is lacking to me and my family. This struggle started to be more apparent when i stepped into middle school. Walking for 20 minutes to school everyday, helping my mother in pasar selling vegetables, and not having alot of pocket money had been my normal routine during half of my middle school. This drives me to learn more about financial stuff just so that i can reduce my parent's burden.


After STPM, my application to get into public university was rejected due to my average STPM result and my ethnicity. But, since the only way that i know that can help me out of poverty is by getting a university degree and get a better paying job, i took 3 years as a gap year to earn enough money to enroll in a private university. Besides that, I also became a part-time Grab Driver to support myself during my university time.

Why I choose to grab this opportunity

After graduating with Mechatronic Engineering Degree, I realized that most engineering fields in Malaysia does not really pays well, especially to fresh graduate. This is apparently due to the abundant of engineers in the job market. My final year in university did made me think a lot about what should I do to move out from the B40 group status into at least M40.


If there is one thing that I learned in university, is that I am quite good in programming. I also learned that the tech industry pays well, with remote job being one of the perks. So when this opportunity came by, I did not hesitate to quit from my part-time job as a kitchen crew. I would like to thank Sigma School for this opportunity and I would do my best to make use of this opportunity.
